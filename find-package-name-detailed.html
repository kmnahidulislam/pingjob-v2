What We're Looking For:
The package name looks like: com.yourcompany.pingjob or com.pingjob or similar.
It's a unique identifier that never changes for your app.
1 App Dashboard - Main Info Section
Google Play Console ‚Üí Your App ‚Üí Dashboard (main page)

Look for:

  • "Package name" field in the app information section
  • Usually displayed prominently near the app name
  • May be in a gray text box or info panel
Example: Package name: com.pingjob
2 App Content - App Details
Google Play Console ‚Üí Your App ‚Üí App content ‚Üí App details

Steps:

In left sidebar, click "App content"
Look for "App details" section
Package name should be listed there
3 Release Management - App Bundle Explorer
Google Play Console ‚Üí Your App ‚Üí Release ‚Üí App bundle explorer

Steps:

Click "Release" in left sidebar
Click "App bundle explorer"
Select your latest release
Package name will be shown in the bundle details
4 URL Method (Easiest!)
Look at your browser URL when viewing your app

The URL contains your package name:

https://play.google.com/console/u/1/developers/XXXXXXX/app/4878611404259928917/app-dashboard

The number in the URL corresponds to your package name.
Tip: From your screenshot, I can see your URL. The long number in your URL can help us identify the package name.
5 Alternative: Check Your Email
Search your email for Google Play Console notifications

Look for emails from Google Play about:

  • App submissions
  • Review notifications
  • Policy updates

These emails often include the package name.

Can't Find It Anywhere?

If the package name isn't visible in any of these locations, here are alternatives:

  • Take more screenshots: Screenshot the "App content" and "Release" sections
  • Share your app URL: Copy the full URL when viewing your app dashboard
  • Check Play Store: Find your app on Google Play Store - the URL there contains the package name
